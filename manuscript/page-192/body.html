Fig. 168. A Rapid-Acting Vise.

Vises for holding wood are of three general styles, (1 those with an upright wooden jaw, Fig. 167, which holds wide pieces of work well. They are now made with an automatic adjusting device by which the jaw and the face of the bench are kept parallel; (2 wooden vises with a horizontal jaw, guided by parallel runners, Fig. 166, and, (3 metal rapid-acting vises, Fig. 168.
