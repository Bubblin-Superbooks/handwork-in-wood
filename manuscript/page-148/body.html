When the plane is not being used, it should rest either on a pillow (a little strip of wood in the bench trough), or on its side. In no case should it be dropped sole down flat on the bench.

The block-plane, Fig. 108, gets its name from the fact that it was first made for planing off the ends of clap-boards, a process called "blocking in".

Fig. 108. Section of Block-Plane.

The names of the parts of the Bailey block-plane are6:

  1. Cutter or bit or plane-iron.
  2. Clamp or lever cup.
  3. Cap-screw.
  4. Adjusting lever.
  5. Adjusting nut.
